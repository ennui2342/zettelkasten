## Results

| Case | Draft | Target (size) | Option A | Step 1+1.5 |
|------|-------|---------------|----------|------------|

| 3 | Emergent Role Specialisation in Homogeneous Pools | Multi-Agent LLM Systems (21,095 chars) | **SYNTHESISE** ✗ | **EDIT** ✓ |

**Option A: 0/3 correct** (target: EDIT or SPLIT)
**Step 1+1.5: 3/3 correct**

## Analysis

Option A failed in every case. The classifier appears to find the draft content topically interesting enough to trigger SYNTHESISE (noting connections across notes) rather than reading the LARGE annotation as a constraint. The annotation competes with the model's natural inclination to classify based on content.

Step 1.5, with its narrow two-option prompt and explicit size framing ("UPDATEing this note would exceed size limits"), produced EDIT reliably for all three cases.

## Decision

Step 1.5 retained. The extra LLM call (fast model, max_tokens=256) is worth the reliability gain.
